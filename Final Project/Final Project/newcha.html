Chattanooga is a beautiful place place for students and tourists alike!

The population of chattanooga was just over 180,000 as of 2020. The city is in the East Tennessee grand division. The city is urban. The median income was 35,000 per household as of 2020. Tourism and higher education are the two largest industries in chattanooga. With excellent public universities in both chattanooga state and the University of chattanooga Tennessee anyone can get to the next step in their career with the opportunities in this city. Tourism is also rich and vibrant. There are many wonderful local restaurants and sites to see in chattanooga. Lookout Mountain, Ruby Falls, the chattanooga Aquarium, and other beautiful sites will keep you and your family busy during your travels. In 1816, John and Lewis Ross arrived and settled the area. In 1839 the State of Tennessee incorporated the city of chattanooga. chattanooga grew into an important city during the civil war because of its immediate access to the Tennessee river and excellent railroad access.

Fun facts about chattanooga!
  1. If you stand at the peak out chattanooga's famous lookout mountain you can see seven states. What a view!

  2. The incline railway which can take you to the top of lookout mountain is the most inclined passenger railway in the world. Let's get inclined!

  3. Mini golf was originally invented in chattanooga! Four!

  4. chattanooga is called "Gig City" for rolling out a next generation municipal fibre network so that its communication infrastructure is ready for the next generation.

  5. There is a famous UFO shaped home built in 1973 by a local named Curtis King sitting amongst the curves of signal mountain.
