Knoxville represents hard working folks being hugged by a striking mountainous geography.

Knoxville, Tennessee has a population of just under 200,000 as of 2020. Knoxville is located in the East Tennessee Grand Division. The city is urban. The median household income is 33,000 per year. Some important industries in Knoxville are trade, transportation, utilities, financial activities, and manufacturing. Knoxville has an incredible history. James White founded the area in 1786 due to excellent geography and access to the Tennessee river. By 1791 the area was renamed to Knoxville and by the 1800s railroad and water access allowed the area to become a massive distribution center for the South. Knoxville is located in the Great Appalachaian Valley somewhere between the Great Smokey Mountains to the east and the Cumberland Plateau to the west. This causes a unique climate that locals experience most of the year because cold air is weakened by the mountains in the winter and hot air is weakened by the mountains in the summer.

Fun facts about Knoxville!
  1. Knoxville was home to one of the greatest Unions supporters during the civil war. William Brownlow was an editor for the Knoxville Whig newspaper and frequently expressed his opinions. That's true bravery!

  2. Knoxville was nicknamed "The Marble City" due to it being a massive hub for marble distribution in the early 1900s. Marble Countertops, anyone?

  3. There are a total of seven lakes that surround Knoxville. Beach day!

  4. Mountain Dew was originally founded in Knoxville! Do the Dew!

  5. Knoxville was the original capital of Tennessee before Nashville was selected for its central location.
